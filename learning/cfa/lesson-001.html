📖 新概念（5 分钟）

什么是 CFA 道德准则？

CFA 协会制定了《道德与职业行为准则》（Code of Ethics and Standards of Professional Conduct），这是 CFA 考试中最重要也最容易丢分的部分。

为什么最重要？

  • 权重最高（15-20%）= 约 36-48 道题
  • 道德调节机制：如果道德部分表现差，即使总分达标也可能不及格
  • 情景题为主：考题都是情景题，不是死记硬背

道德准则的六大组成部分

序号 组成部分 核心理解
1 以诚信、胜任、勤勉、尊重和道德方式行事 你的行为 = 行业声誉
2 将投资行业的诚信和客户利益置于自身利益之上 客户永远第一
3 运用合理判断并保持独立的专业判断 独立思考，不盲从
4 自己践行并鼓励他人以专业和道德方式行事 以身作则
5 维护全球资本市场的诚信和活力 不操纵市场
6 保持并提升专业能力 永不停止学习

💡 记忆技巧： 把这六条想象成"一个金融从业者的做人底线"——对客户的底线、对自己的底线、对市场的底线。

📝 例题解析（7 分钟）

例题 1

情景： 张分析师是基金经理。他发现自己的个人账户可以买入一只股票，这只股票他正准备大量推荐给基金客户。他应该怎么做？

👉 点击查看答案 **分析：** - 根据"客户利益至上"原则，**客户订单必须优先于个人交易** - 张必须确保客户订单先于个人订单执行 - 这叫做 **"Front Running"（抢跑交易）**，是严重违规 **答案：** ❌ 张分析师不能先于客户买入

例题 2

情景： 李分析师在一次行业会议上无意中听到某公司 CEO 透露下季度业绩超预期。她可以基于此调整研究报告吗？

👉 点击查看答案 **分析：** - CEO 无意透露 = **重大非公开信息（MNPI）** - 基于 MNPI 做决策 = **内幕交易**，违反"市场诚信"原则 - 即使信息是"无意中"听到的，也不可以使用 **答案：** ❌ 不可以，这是重大非公开信息

自测题 1

自测题 2

A. 不需要披露，这是个人关系 B. 只在客户问起时才披露 C. 主动披露利益冲突 ✅ D. 等股票涨了再披露

自测题 3

A. 在研究报告中使用公开数据 B. 拒绝基于内幕信息做交易 C. 向客户保证"这只股票一定涨" ✅ D. 定期审查自己的投资决策

📖 New Concepts (5 minutes)

What is the CFA Code of Ethics?

The CFA Institute has established a Code of Ethics and Standards of Professional Conduct, which is the most important and most frequently tested section of the CFA exam.

Why is it so important?

  • Highest weight (15-20%) = approximately 36-48 questions
  • Ethics "bump" rule: If your Ethics performance is weak, you may fail even with a passing total score
  • Scenario-based: Questions test practical judgment, not rote memorization

The Six Components of the Code of Ethics

# Component Key Idea
1 Act with integrity, competence, diligence, respect, and in an ethical manner Your behavior reflects on the profession
2 Place the integrity of the investment profession and clients' interests above your own Clients come first, always
3 Use reasonable care and exercise independent professional judgment Think independently; don't follow the crowd
4 Practice and encourage others to practice ethically Lead by example
5 Promote the integrity and viability of global capital markets Don't manipulate markets
6 Maintain and improve professional competence Never stop learning

💡 Memory Tip: Think of these six components as the "professional bottom line" — your baseline, the client's baseline, and the market's baseline.

📝 Example Questions (7 minutes)

Example 1

Scenario: Analyst Zhang is a fund manager. He realizes his personal account can buy a stock that he is about to recommend heavily to fund clients. What should he do?

👉 Click to reveal answer **Analysis:** - Under the "clients first" principle, **client orders must take priority over personal trades** - Zhang must ensure client orders are executed before his personal orders - This is called **"Front Running"** — a serious violation **Answer:** ❌ Zhang must NOT buy before his clients

Example 2

Scenario: Analyst Li overhears a company CEO unintentionally revealing that next quarter's earnings will beat expectations at an industry conference. Can she adjust her research report based on this?

👉 Click to reveal answer **Analysis:** - CEO's unintentional disclosure = **Material Nonpublic Information (MNPI)** - Trading or acting on MNPI = **Insider Trading**, violates "market integrity" - Even if the information was overheard "by accident," you still cannot use it **Answer:** ❌ No, this is material nonpublic information

Question 1

A. Recommend the product with the highest commission, because the bank needs revenue B. Recommend the product most suitable for the client's risk profile ✅ C. Recommend the product he holds the most of personally D. Recommend the product with the best recent performance

Question 2

Analyst Zhao discovers that his friend holds a stock that he is recommending to clients. He needs to:

A. No disclosure needed — it's a personal relationship B. Only disclose if the client asks C. Proactively disclose the conflict of interest ✅ D. Disclose after the stock goes up

Question 3

Which of the following actions violates the CFA Code of Ethics?

A. Using public data in a research report B. Refusing to trade based on material nonpublic information C. Guaranteeing to a client that "this stock will definitely go up" ✅ D. Regularly reviewing his own investment decisions

✅ Today's Summary (3 minutes)

Key Takeaways:

  1. The Six Components of the CFA Code of Ethics
  2. Clients' interests first is the core principle
  3. Insider trading, front running, and guaranteeing returns are all explicit violations

Answer Key: 1-B 2-C 3-C
